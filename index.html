Values

Towards the end of 2020 I had an opportunity to participate in an exercise:

Imagine it is your funeral and people give speeches - what do they say about you, do you like what they say? And then focus on the most important things/values in your life - what are those, can you rank/prioritise them?

The list I had come up with was subsequently amended after the birth of my son and currently looks like this:

Family

Simply be an awesome dad and partner.

Financial security

Always know your financial situation, budget well, justify expenses. Look for opportunities to increase your wealth. Make sure you are paid fairly.

Be truthful to yourself

Stay true to who you are, what you say and what you believe in. Don’t worry about pleasing other people. Don’t live by someone else’s standards or rules. Don’t allow others to twist your arm to do something that makes you feel unhappy.

Put a dent in the universe

Achieve something truly great on a global scale, participate in big transformational initiatives that introduce revolutionary products and/or services benefiting the whole human race.

Health

Take care of your body and mind. Sleep enough, eat healthy and stay on top of any psychological issues you might be experiencing.

Journey

After experiencing a plateau for a prolonged period of time and finally reaching the burnout stage in my role as a Business Analyst, I have decided to shake this off and go back to who I was a few years back - full of energy, ambitious and always pushing forward.

At around the same time I had gone through the funeral exercise so I had already identified what were the most important things in my life. In the meantime, I also read the book Elon Musk: A Mission to Save the World by Anna Crowley Redding and recalled the mind-blowing Neuralink presentation (my medical vocation calling from the past?). Both have greatly inspired me - this is the goal I set for myself:

In 5 years, I will work for a company that implements bold ideas that make a huge difference in people’s lives and I will contribute to these implementations … or I will set up one such company.

I concluded that the best chance at accomplishing my goal was to shift my career to a software engineer.

Furthermore, I will need to:

  • find myself in a collaborative and innovative environment
  • be allowed to make my own choices, execute my own learning and fail forward
  • be provided not only with guidance and support but also with challenging tasks
  • be allowed to explore different fields to find the area of my deeper interest

Resume

Education

Medicine 2003-2007

Yep, my parents wanted me to be a doctor. I had no strong preference after high school (and probably no say either). It took me 4 years to gather enough courage to tell my parents I wanted to do something else. Until the very end, I was one of the top students (with a scholarship for high grade average) which probably made it even harder to do what I needed to do. It was unbelievably hard work, sleeping 3 hours a day for the first 2 years, but it gave me a gigantic boost of self-confidence - if I could be one of the bests at something I wasn't strongly passionate about, then there is probably not a lot of things in the world that I cannot excel at. I just needed to find the thing I truly loved.

Computer Science 2007-2012

Sparked by a book HTML4 by Dave Tylor in early high school. With the financial support of only my mother, I had to rely on a student loan and summer jobs in the UK. I studied online (probably the best way to study for me due to its flexibility and my self-determination) and in English (I was able to use it later to prove my English eligibility for NZ residence). It took me 5 years to graduate - with 2 majors (Databases and Business Applications Programming) and 1st class honours - as I was working at the same time. I loved every minute of it! I was soaking in coding, algorithms, math! The only formal studies I have actually completed ;-) Feel free to check out the GitHub repo of the final project.

MBA 2012-2017

I wanted to do something more prestigious than just a regular Master's degree. I wanted to win the rat race. I was already working as a BA/PM so the direction felt right. I chose an online MBA from an accredited university in the UK. With my Polish salary, I wasn’t able to sustain the recommended pace - I could only do half of the modules at any one time. With a move to NZ and a bunch of other things on my plate and an upcoming change of curriculum, I decided to take Postgraduate Certificate only - as a consolation prize it was graduation with distinction. I did learn a lot across multiple domains. And it was awesome to see Six Sigma in action at the Jaguar factory in Birmingham!

Work Experience

First professional job. Started as a front-end dev, also BA/PM tasks, later 2IC for the awesome front-end dev team. Eye-opening secondment to Singapore for Internet banking replacement project - received Instant Recognition reward for commitment and dedication. Great life lesson. Made friendships for life. Responsible for introducing mobile banking at Alior Bank, wearing different hats: from PM to BA to … writing the script for the promotional video. Felt great to see my apps climbing in AppStore and Google Play rankings - one of the greatest successes in my professional career!

Fiserv 2013-2014 (1 year)

Worked as a BA on the Bank of Ireland iOS and Android apps. Did market analysis of Fiserv’s Internet and mobile banking products vs solutions on the Polish market. Finally, the most interesting of them - I supported a revenue enhancement project for one of the banks in Poland where I learnt how exactly banks earn big bucks on customers.

Kiwibank 2014-2017 (3 years)

The first employer in NZ. Joined one of the coolest teams I have ever worked in (InTouch). Made great friendships. Learnt Scrum. The team was so efficient at it. This kind of spoiled it for me - no future team I worked in was able to match the same standard of Scrum. Later I trialled as a Technical Delivery Lead. Lot more pressure, longer hours, had to solve people’s problems. Apparently I was successful, but I didn’t enjoy it. Moved to another team (IT Payments) as a Tech BA. Participated in a few compliance pieces and the replacement of the core banking system. As a 2IC I helped to implement Agile practices in the team. Won IT Payments hero award 3 times and was nominated for Quarterly IT award.

Victoria University 2017-2018 (2 years)

My Project Manager summarised it perfectly: one of a very few BAs in town that could pull it off on their own. A migration project with such a broad scope. And one BA. That’s when I realised I loved looking at data, searching for anomalies, linking entities. Very hands-on technical analysis. Also helped developers to code behaviours in JavaScript in MS Dynamics CRM. Practised drawing process maps. Smoke tested every single change. Introduced Kanban process flow for incoming work. Toward the end, touched on CRM-Marketo (marketing automation software) integration. I thoroughly enjoyed it!

ACC 2019-2021 (2 years)

Workplace Injury Prevention Subsidies - an awesome initiative of making NZ a safer workplace. Worked across teams to implement the technical solution behind it. BA on the project but with responsibilities spanning outside of my core role (has become a norm over the years). Scrum Master for the CRM team. Coordinated Production releases. Taught myself Power BI to introduce reports on the performance of subsidies. Later moved to another team as a Tech BA where I experienced SAFe and learnt a few more things about integration. This is also where I did the funeral exercise that started the shift.
